## Evening — #49 suburbs + density gradient, increments 1–4 (feature/suburbs-v2)

Kicked off the #49 rebuild on `feature/suburbs-v2` (off dev, post-#55 ship).
Created [[decision-density-gradient-model]] — the load-bearing choices:

- **Density field** (`lib/seed/density.ts`): absolute-distance Clark's-law
  falloff (NOT extent-normalised — bigger tiers ADD rural/fringe bands),
  4 bands (core/suburban/rural/fringe), seeded angular harmonics for organic
  band edges. Two layers: radial (pre-district, for road gating) +
  per-district (jitter + character floors, for buildings/lamps).
- **/plan density layer**: band-filled cells + radial contour rings (toggle,
  default off).
- **Buildings**: ~150 m hash-seeded development-cell dropout (whole-parcel
  gaps, kept fabric stays FILLED — the spike's per-lot skip inverted),
  archetype tilt + size shrink toward the edge, interior fill fades to yards.
- **Streetlights**: spacing-not-brightness (×1→2.5 stretch), one-sided
  staggered zig-zag past suburbAmount 0.35, never zero; main roads exempt.
- **Roads**: arterial seed gate + separation taper (through-lines terminate at
  band edges; survivors = radial spokes), street-sep ramp (tight in suburbs so
  subdivision reads, country lanes in rural). Found via lamp telemetry that
  the arterial grid — not the lamps — was what kept the rim uniformly lit.
- Verified each increment: gate1 PASS throughout; per-band counts
  (e.g. City tier: buildings ~870/610/190/15 per km² across bands; lamps
  ~1100/600/230/60) + /plan and fog-free scene captures in
  `samples/verify49/`. Tier-3 default orbit look unregressed.
