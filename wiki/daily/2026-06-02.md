## Traffic head/tail colour — elevation-independent (follow-up #45)

User reported car lights flipping white↔red on *elevation* change, not azimuth:
at azim 230 on a parallel highway the colour switched between elev 35 and 73. Root
cause in `lib/shaders/traffic.ts`: the top-down `aHead` fallback blended by
`sin²(elev)` (0.33 at 35°, 0.91 at 73°), so any downward tilt dragged the
camera-relative answer toward the baked flow. Fix: decide colour from the
**horizontal** bearing only — `normalize(uViewDir.xz)` (camera orbit azimuth) vs
the car heading flattened to XZ — so elevation drops out entirely. Broadside (~90°)
sits mid red/white with a `smoothstep(±0.2)` easing band (~±11.5°). Dropped the
`vert`/`aHead` fallback (attribute now unused). Shipped to main `878a3f2`, deploy green.

## Highway research pass (#13) — created [[highway-network-references]]

Ran the deep-research harness (110 agents, 27 sources, 25 claims verified, 20
confirmed / 5 refuted) on freeway network design for #13. Landed
[[highway-network-references]] (linked from [[research]] MOC). Key load-bearing
params: strict functional hierarchy + zero lot frontage; ~1 mi (1600 m) urban
interchange cadence; service interchange = one ~1.5 km module; type selection by

tiered night lighting (CFL/CIL/PIL) + high-mast clusters at cores vs single-pole
cadence elsewhere + paired ribbons on divided carriageways. Confirms #13 belongs at
**Metro scale** (only ~1–2 interchange spacings fit our 3 km City tier — parked
behind #14). **Open before coding:** metro freeway COUNT + parallel-freeway spacing,
and system-interchange footprint dims — neither settled by this pass.
