# After Dark — Starry Night (original Mac screensaver)

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SRQYFTPV2W4 — YouTube capture of the original
Berkeley Systems *After Dark* "Starry Night" module running on an old Macintosh.

**Added:** 2026-05-31

This is the **primary anchor reference** for the whole project — Starry Night (working title)
is a modernized homage to this exact screensaver (see `docs/PRD.md` and root `CLAUDE.md`). Where
[[streets-of-rage-intro]] sets the *palette / skyline composition*, this clip is the reference for
the *ambient behaviour and pacing*.

> fill in after watching if useful.

## What to study from it

Observational prompts to revisit against the live scene — annotate with specifics after watching:

- **Pacing** — how slow is the ambient motion? The city should feel still + sleepy, not animated.
- **Stars / sky** — star density, twinkle cadence, any shooting stars or drift; how much of the
  frame is sky vs city.
- **Skyline silhouette** — building proportions, how low the city sits, foreground vs back rows.
- **Window lighting** — which windows are lit, warm vs cool, how/whether they flicker or wake over
  time (our intro wake-cycle + TV-blue flicker should echo this).
- **Colour** — overall night palette; compare to our ACES/sRGB output + the SoR navy.
- **Camera** — is it a fixed frame or any slow drift? (We use a slow orbit; the original may be static.)

## Relevance to current work

- Validates the **ambient, low-motion** target — informs intro pacing + the streetlight/traffic
  ease-in timing.
- A behaviour benchmark for "does this read as *Starry Night*" beyond just the still-image palette.
