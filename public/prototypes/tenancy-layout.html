Tenancy Layout throwaway prototype

How one building's facade subdivides into tenant regions — spans of bays × floors, per face. Toggle Night lights to preview the lit look: each small storefront gets its own glow (warm, fluorescent, neon), while a department store reads as uniform bright white. The question: do the parcels and their lights read as believable places?

Hover any parcel to inspect its span…

Facades are drawn unfolded: the four faces (N / E / S / W) laid flat, ground floor at the bottom. Tenant type colours each parcel by who occupies it; Night lights shows what it would emit — storefront lighting is deliberately mixed (a main/high-street trait), department stores uniform and bright, offices mostly dark-and-cool, homes warm and scattered. Rules are v1 and will get more granular. Known simplification: storefronts wrap the whole ground ring — really they'd cluster on the street face (a face-aware refinement for later). Nothing here is wired to the app; it exists to tune tenancyLayout() and the light character by eye before porting into the seed + shader.
